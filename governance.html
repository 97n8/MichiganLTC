How the project is governed.

Seven living registers, a signature-routing workflow, written-addendum control, and documented escalation paths keep the corridor reviewable, transferable, and accountable.

The registers

Seven registers, one source of truth.

Each register is a maintained markdown record in the governed workspace. Together they answer: what was decided, by whom, on what authority, under which assumptions, and at what risk.

Governance registers maintained in the project workspace.
Register What it answers Key fields
Decision log What was decided, by whom, on what authority Date, decision, decider, source/authority, affected site, status, follow-up
Authority map Who can decide, sign, or represent what Principals, PublicLogic scope, site partners, grant/permitting/finance contacts
Signature routing Who signs which materials, and how approval is documented Document type, authorized signer, review path, execution record
Assumptions register What we're assuming, and the risk if wrong Assumption, source, confidence, risk if wrong, owner, verify-by date
Risk register What could go wrong, and who owns the mitigation Risk, category, site, likelihood, impact, mitigation, owner, status
Change log What changed in the working structure, and why Date, change, reason, source, approved by, files affected
Issue escalation path Where each category of issue routes Documentation, funding, site, partner, regulatory, financial, legal routes
Signature routing

Five steps from draft to documented approval.

Identify

Document type, purpose, and affected site or workstream.

Classify

Informational, operational, legal, financial, grant, regulatory, or external-facing.

Confirm signer

Authorized signer per the authority map and written agreements.

Route for review

Drafts circulate for review before any signature request.

Record approval

Authority, date, version, governing record, and storage location.

Written addenda are required when…

  • A new site is proposed
  • Scope expands beyond currently authorized work
  • Authority changes for sign-off or representation
  • New obligations, commitments, or controlled disclosures are introduced
  • The workspace is asked to support work outside documented authorization

Escalation, by issue type

  • Documentation → document owner or workstream lead
  • Funding → funding lead + approval authority before external communication
  • Site → site lead; update site file and risk register
  • Partner → documented relationship owner
  • Regulatory → designated regulatory contact; drafts are not determinations
  • Financial → authorized finance contact before use in deliverables
  • Legal / professional → stop informal circulation; route to authorized reviewer immediately
