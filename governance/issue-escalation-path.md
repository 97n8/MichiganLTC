# Issue Escalation Path

Use this guide to route issues clearly and quickly without overstating authority.

## Documentation issues
If a document is missing, inconsistent, outdated, or unsupported, route first to the document owner or workstream lead. If the issue affects a decision, submission, or external-facing use, log it in the relevant governance or deliverable tracker.

## Funding issues
If a grant, funding opportunity, budget, or eligibility issue could affect timing, compliance, or positioning, route it to the funding lead and the relevant approval authority before external communication.

## Site issues
If a site-specific issue affects Coleman or Flint operations, assumptions, coordination, or readiness, route it to the site lead and update the related site file and risk register.

## Partner issues
If a partner request, dependency, or disagreement affects scope, authority, data sharing, or timelines, route it through the documented relationship owner and record the issue status.

## Regulatory issues
If a permitting, zoning, environmental, or compliance question arises, route it to the designated regulatory contact and do not represent draft interpretations as final determinations.

## Financial issues
If a financial assumption, investor-facing statement, budget change, or match requirement is in question, escalate to the authorized finance contact before using the information in any deliverable.

## Legal or professional issues
If an issue touches contracts, legal exposure, representation authority, confidentiality, or regulated professional judgment, stop informal circulation and route it to the appropriate authorized reviewer immediately.
